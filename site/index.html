Make sense of a messy list without uploading it

Gistmap is a free tool for anyone sitting on a pile of short text they need to understand fast: a product manager reading a column of survey free-responses, a researcher triaging interview notes, a founder skimming feature requests, or anyone who dumped a hundred to-dos into a note and wants to see the themes. You paste the lines, press Map it, and Gistmap groups the ones that mean similar things and gives each group a plain-language name. The point is to see the shape of a list in seconds instead of reading every row twice.

It works because a real sentence-embedding model runs inside the page. Each line is turned into a vector by all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (about 7 MB, quantized) through Transformers.js on WebAssembly. Gistmap then clusters those vectors with k-means, picks a sensible number of clusters for you, labels each one from the terms that make it distinctive, and projects everything onto a 2D star map you can hover, re-cluster, and reproject. Because the model runs on your own machine, the text you paste is never sent to a server. The only network request is the one-time model download, and you can confirm that in your browser's network tab.

How to use it

  1. Open the app and paste your text, one item per line, or load a sample.
  2. Press Map it. The model loads once, then embeds and clusters your lines.
  3. Hover a star to read its line; click a constellation in the legend to isolate it.
  4. Drag the constellation-count slider to split or merge groups, and reproject for a fresh layout.
  5. Export the labeled result as JSON or CSV when you want to keep it.

Frequently asked questions

Does my text get uploaded anywhere?
No. Embedding, clustering, labeling, and rendering all happen in your browser. The only thing downloaded is the model file, once per tab.
How many lines can I map?
Gistmap maps up to 500 lines per run and tells you if it dropped any past that. A few hundred short lines cluster in roughly two seconds after the model has loaded.
Do I need an account, an API key, or a paid plan?
None of them. Gistmap is a single static page. Open it and paste; there is nothing to sign up for and no key to configure.
How are the cluster names chosen?
Each cluster is named from the terms that appear often inside it but rarely across the rest of your list, so you get a label like "billing, refund, invoice" rather than a number.
What kind of text works best?
Short, self-contained lines: tasks, tweets, survey answers, bookmarks, feedback, or notes. The more each line stands on its own, the cleaner the map.
